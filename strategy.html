3 Shot Poker âœ…

Fold unless your cards are a pair, your cards are the same suit, or if they form the inside of two different potential straights (like how 4 and 5 can make 345 or 456, but Ace and King can only make one potential straight).

If Straights pay 5:1 on your pay table, don't fold if your cards form any part of a potential straight (for example 4 and 6 are separated but have the potential to make 456).

Crazy 4 Poker

Until extremely recently, the general rule here was to fold if you didn't have a K+Q+8+4 or better. Now with modern computers we can find the true optimal strategy.

K+Q+8+4+3 and K+Q+7+6+5 should be folded if there are two suits, and K and Q are different suits.

You should fold on K+Q+8+4+2 if King and Queen are different suits

This might be the complete optimal strategy, I am in the process of verifying!

Dakota Duel Draw âœ…

Play if one card is nine or higher, Play if your cards form a pair, and Play if your cards are the same suit (except for 3-8, 2-8, and 2-7), otherwise Fold.

Do this correctly, and you will have a house edge of between 2.57%-5.29%, depending on the pocket bonus pay table.

Four Card Poker

Simple Strategy

Raise x3 with a pair of tens or better, raise x1 with a pair of twos or better, and to fold otherwise.

Advanced Strategy

Pair of Aces or better: Bet x3

Pair of Kings: Bet x3, except bet x1 against an Ace and you don't have an Ace nor 4.

Pair of Queens or Jacks: Bet x3, except bet x1 if the dealer's card outranks pair your pair rank and does not match a singleton in your hand.

Pair of 9s or 10s: Bet x3, except bet x1 if dealer card outranks your pair rank.

Pair of 8s: Bet x1, except bet x3 against a 2

Pair of 4s thru 7s: Bet x1

Pair of 3s: Bet x1, except fold against a Jack if your highest kicker is a 10 or lower

Pair of 2s or AKQ: Fold, except bet x1 if dealer card matches a rank in your hand

AKJT: Fold, except bet x1 against a Jack

AKJ9 or lower: Fold

I have heard rumors that gambling expert James Grosjean has developed an even more advanced strategy.

My analysis also shows that you should actually bet x1 with AKJ9 if the dealer is showing an Ace or King :)

Heads Up Hold'Em

Raise x4 if you have an Ace, if you have a King with 5-Queen, if you have a Queen with a 8-Jack, or a Jack with a 10.

Additionally raise x4 if you have a suited King with anything, a Queen with 6 or 7, or a Jack with 8 or 9.

If you need a flush or better to win on the Ante, fold a 2 with a 3-7 if they have different suits, or an unsuited 3+4.
